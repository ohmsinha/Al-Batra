# Al Batra — SEO Strategy

A working document for ranking Al Batra Management Consultancy on the searches that matter in the UAE ISO certification market. Update quarterly.

## Charter

**Objective**: Become the default search result for "ISO certification UAE" and the long-tail variants UAE businesses actually search for — across Google, Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity and Claude.

**Audience**: UAE quality, operations, and HSE leaders procuring a consultancy to implement a management system (not buying a certificate). Secondary: tender admins responding to government RFPs that require ISO certification as pre-qualification.

**Coverage area**: UAE-wide (Sharjah HQ), with priority on Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah; secondary on Ajman, RAK, Fujairah, UAQ; opportunistic on GCC.

**Languages**: English (primary) + Arabic (RTL, parallel surface).

| Intent | What ranks |

| Informational (Tier 3) | Article-format insights with author bio, dated publication, citations, FAQs in body. Schema: `Article` + `FAQPage`. |
| Local (Tier 1 with emirate modifier) | Service × emirate landing pages. Localized intro (≤200 words direct answer), emirate-specific notes (regulator, common sectors), shared service body. Schema: `Service` with `areaServed` city. |
| Programmatic long-tail (Tier 4) | Industry × standard combo pages. Sector-specific copy, regulator references, sector-specific case study, FAQs. |

## Site-wide SEO rules

**Title pattern**:
- Home: `ISO Certification Consultants in the UAE — Al Batra Management Consultancy`
- Service: `[Standard] Certification in the UAE — Fixed-Fee Implementation by Al Batra`
- Service × Emirate: `[Standard] Consultant in [Emirate] — Bilingual, Fixed-Fee · Al Batra`
- Industry: `ISO Certification for [Industry] in the UAE — Sector-Specific Implementation · Al Batra`
- Case study: `Case Study · [Title] — Al Batra`
- Insight: `[Article title] — Al Batra Insights`

**Meta description pattern**:
- Lead with primary keyword in first 60 chars
- Include the trust signal (fixed-fee, bilingual, 600+ clients, Sharjah HQ)
- Soft CTA at the end
- 150–160 chars

**H1 pattern**: One per page, contains or paraphrases the primary query. Different from `<title>` wording.

**Internal linking**:
- Every service page links to: 2-3 related services, 1-2 industries that commonly need it, 1-2 case studies if available, the methodology page, and `/pricing/`.
- Every industry page links to: the 3-5 most-applicable services, 1-2 case studies in that sector, and the home.
- Every case study links to: the relevant service pages and the industry page.
- The home links to: services hub, industries hub, methodology, case studies, contact.

**Image alt text**: Descriptive, includes service or industry context where natural. Decorative images get empty alt.

**URL pattern**: Lowercase, hyphens, no dates unless time-bound, ≤60 chars where possible.

## E-E-A-T strategy

Google and AI engines heavily weight Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — especially for YMYL-adjacent topics like compliance. We need to demonstrate it.

1. **Author bios on every insight** — name, role, years in practice, sectors covered, linked Person schema with `sameAs` to LinkedIn.
2. **Case studies with specifics** — every case has metrics, timelines, and the certification body's findings, not generic outcomes.
3. **About page depth** — founder, leadership team, ten-year history, Sharjah Municipality approval, 600+ organisations served.
4. **Citations** — when blog posts reference the standard text or UAE regulation, link to the original (ISO.org, MOCCAE, Sharjah Municipality, DCAS).
5. **Original commentary** — articles take positions, not just summaries. AI engines cite opinions with specifics.

**Template discipline** (from `programmatic-seo` skill):
- Above-the-fold answer: emirate-specific intro that names the emirate, the standard, the typical UAE regulator touchpoints, and the engagement model in 150–200 words.
- Below-fold: shared service body (reused from the canonical service page), then emirate-specific context (regulator notes, sector mix, typical Stage 2 cadence).
- Distinctive per page: emirate name woven into copy, sector overlap noted (e.g., Sharjah's manufacturing concentration, DIFC for ISO 27001), specific UAE regulatory references where they exist.
- Schema: `Service` with `areaServed` set to the emirate, breadcrumb showing the hierarchy.
- Internal linking: each emirate page links to the parent service, the coverage page, related industries, and the home.

**What we don't do**: spin up every 27 standards × 7 emirates = 189 pages. That's thin-content territory. Top 5 × top 4 = 20 high-quality pages is enough; we can expand later if these earn traffic.

## Local SEO

UAE-wide consultancy needs strong local signals to win in local-intent searches.

1. **LocalBusiness schema** on home with full NAP, opening hours, `geo`, and `areaServed` list of all 7 emirates plus GCC.
2. **Google Business Profile** (off-platform action) — claim, verify, optimize with photos of Sharjah office, services, hours, contact, reviews. Outside scope of this code change.
3. **NAP consistency** — name, address, phone identical across the site, GBP, social, citations.
4. **Local citations** (off-platform action) — UAE Yellow Pages, Yelp UAE, Dubai Chamber of Commerce directory, Sharjah Chamber. Outside this code change.
5. **Reviews** — Google reviews on GBP, embedded testimonials with `Review` schema (cautious: only with explicit client consent; we have `Review` schema-ready in our markup but not enabled until consent is secured).
