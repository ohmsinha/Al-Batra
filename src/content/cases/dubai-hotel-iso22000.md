## Operating context

The group operates three UAE properties under an international brand standard — Dubai (city), Sharjah (resort), and Abu Dhabi (city-airport corridor). Each property runs an outlet kitchen and shares supply from a central kitchen operated as a fourth, non-customer-facing site. F&B revenue represents roughly 35% of group revenue, and the central kitchen supplies all outlet menus.

The previous FSMS was implemented property by property by a different consultancy. Stage 2 had passed in 2022, but the 2023 surveillance audit raised 11 non-conformities — the certification body's auditor reported "site-by-site interpretation of identical clauses, no common process language."

## What we changed

Rather than rebuild four FSMSs in parallel, we treated the central kitchen as the master site. The HACCP study, prerequisite programmes, supplier qualification and management review process all sit at the central kitchen. Each outlet kitchen received a site annex covering only the variations from the master — outlet-specific menus, plating procedures, allergen disclosure, customer-facing handover.

This collapsed roughly 280 documents into 140, with no loss of audit evidence. Internal-auditor training was delivered as a single cohort of eight, drawn from across all three properties, so audits could cross-cover sites and surface site-to-site variation.

## The audit room

Stage 2 surveillance ran across the three properties over five working days. The certification body's lead auditor flagged the new structure as "the cleanest multi-site FSMS we've audited this cycle" — no major NCs and three minor NCs all closed within 14 days.

## What the group did next

The pattern has since been extended to two additional UAE properties (Ajman + Ras Al Khaimah) by the group's QHSE team — without re-engaging Al Batra. The handover is what we aim for on every engagement.
