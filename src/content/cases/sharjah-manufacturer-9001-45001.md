## Operating context

The operator runs a 14,000 m² fabrication facility in Sharjah, with structural steel, sheet metal and finishing lines. The workforce is approximately 220, predominantly Arabic-speaking on the shop floor, with English-speaking management.

The driver was a federal tender prequalification deadline: ISO 9001 and ISO 45001 certificates issued before week 11, or the bid was inadmissible. The operator had pre-existing documentation but no integration and no internal-audit history.

## What we changed

Two streams in parallel:

- The **quality stream** rebuilt around the operator's existing process map. Most existing procedures were retained with light edits, version-controlled, and slotted into a single manual aligned to ISO 9001:2015.
- The **safety stream** translated the existing Arabic HSE procedures into English without losing operational language, then integrated them as the OHSMS half of the manual. Risk assessments were re-conducted at the workstation level with the supervisors.

Both streams used a shared management review and a single internal-audit cycle, conducted bilingually with two Arabic-speaking and two English-speaking auditors from the client team. The audit ran for two and a half days against both standards simultaneously.

## The audit room

Stage 2 ran for three days. The certification body's lead auditor was Arabic-speaking and split the audit across both languages — Arabic interviews at the workstation level, English with management. One minor NC was raised on manual version control (an indexing inconsistency) and closed within 48 hours.

## What changed for the operator

The certificate was issued in week 10, four days inside the tender window. The bid was admitted, and was awarded six weeks later. The same documentation is now used as the operator's working management system; no further consulting engagement was requested.
