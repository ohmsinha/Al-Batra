## What ISO 9001 covers, in practice

ISO 9001:2015 is a generic management standard — meaning it applies to any organisation, regardless of size or sector. It asks you to define the context of your organisation, the needs of interested parties, the processes that produce your product or service, and the way you measure, audit and continually improve them.

For a UAE organisation, an ISO 9001 certificate is most often required by:

- Tender prequalification, especially for government and semi-government work
- Free-zone licensing categories that reward quality-certified operators
- International buyers who require ISO 9001 as a supplier baseline
- Group policy, where a regional headquarters has standardised on the certificate

## How Al Batra implements ISO 9001

We work in five phases, scoped and priced up front, with weekly on-site presence and a single lead consultant assigned for the engagement.

1. **Gap analysis** — a clause-by-clause walk-through against ISO 9001:2015. Findings are logged with the responsible owner, the action required and the target close-out date. The output is a written report you can act on without us.
2. **Documentation** — the manual, procedures, work instructions, forms and records, written for your operation. Bilingual where the certification body or controlling-language clause requires it.
3. **Implementation** — on-site rollout, awareness sessions for staff, internal auditor training and an evidence trail your auditors can follow.
4. **Internal audit** — a full internal audit cycle against the standard, with corrective actions tracked through to closure and management review minutes prepared.
5. **Certification body support** — we stay with you through Stage 1 and Stage 2, respond to auditor questions on site, and handle non-conformity closure and the first surveillance audit.

## What you receive at the end of the engagement

## Common questions

**Can we use our existing procedures?**
Yes. We will not rewrite for the sake of rewriting. The aim is a management system that is yours — and that you can keep running after Al Batra leaves.

**Do we need to certify every site?**
No. The scope of certification is something we agree at proposal stage. Multi-site UAE clients often phase certification — head office and one operational site first, then expansion.

**What happens after Stage 2?**
We support the first surveillance audit (usually twelve months later) as part of the fixed fee. After that, your team can run the system independently or retain us for an annual support cycle.
