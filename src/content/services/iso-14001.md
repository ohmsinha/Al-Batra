## ISO 14001 in a UAE context

Environmental regulation in the UAE has tightened steadily since 2018. Federal-level laws on waste management, single-use plastics and emissions are now joined by emirate-level rules that touch most operating organisations — particularly in manufacturing, energy, hospitality and logistics. ISO 14001 gives you a framework for identifying which of those regulations apply to you, demonstrating compliance, and continually improving.

## Our ISO 14001 implementation

- **Aspect-impact register** — every activity, product and service mapped to its environmental aspects and the impacts they create, with a significance rating you can defend in audit.
- **Legal compliance evaluation** — a register of applicable UAE federal and emirate environmental laws, with the evidence demonstrating each is met.
- **Operational controls** — procedures and work instructions for high-impact activities, written for the people doing the work.
- **Emergency preparedness** — response plans for environmental incidents (spills, releases, fire).
- **Performance evaluation** — KPIs, monitoring, internal audit and management review.
- **Certification body support** — Stage 1 and Stage 2 attendance, non-conformity closure.

## Frequently combined with

ISO 14001 is commonly implemented alongside ISO 9001 (quality) and ISO 45001 (occupational health and safety) as an integrated management system (IMS). When two or three standards are in scope, we run a single engagement with a shared documentation set — one manual, one internal audit programme, one management review.
