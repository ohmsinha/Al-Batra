The HACCP-versus-ISO 22000 question is the most common food safety enquiry we field — from hotel general managers, from central kitchen operators, from independent restaurant groups expanding from one outlet to a chain. The honest, short answer is this:

**HACCP is the methodology. ISO 22000 is the management system that runs HACCP.**

You do not choose between them. If you operate a food business in the UAE, you almost certainly need HACCP-compliant operations as a baseline. ISO 22000 is the certifiable management system that includes HACCP plus everything else the standard requires.

The longer answer — and the one that matters for the decision UAE operators actually face — is below.

## HACCP, defined briefly

HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) is a food safety methodology that originated in the US space programme in the 1960s and has been the global baseline for food safety thinking since the 1990s. The core idea: identify the points in a food production or service process where a hazard (biological, chemical, physical) can be introduced or proliferate; control those points; monitor them; and respond when monitoring shows a deviation.

HACCP is a methodology, not a management system standard. You can implement HACCP without ISO 22000. You cannot implement ISO 22000 without HACCP — the standard requires it.

## ISO 22000, defined briefly

ISO 22000 is the international standard for food safety management systems. It includes HACCP as a core requirement, layered with:

- **Prerequisite programmes (PRPs)**: the operational conditions that have to be in place before HACCP can work — cleaning, pest control, supplier qualification, water quality, staff hygiene, equipment maintenance
- **Management system requirements**: context, leadership, planning, support, performance evaluation, improvement — the framework all ISO management system standards share
- **Documentation and evidence**: documented procedures, records, internal audits, management reviews, corrective action processes

In short: HACCP is the food safety logic. ISO 22000 is the documented, audited management system that operates HACCP and proves it works.

## What UAE regulators actually require

UAE federal food safety law and emirate-level municipality requirements set the regulatory baseline.

**Sharjah Municipality** requires food handlers to hold GHP (Good Hygiene Practice) certificates from approved providers and expects HACCP-aligned operations. ISO 22000 is not required by Sharjah Municipality for licensing, but operators implementing ISO 22000 satisfy and exceed the underlying requirements.

**Dubai Municipality** operates Person In Charge (PIC) and Food Code requirements, with HACCP-aligned operations expected for higher-risk food businesses. Hotels, central kitchens, and large catering operators are under increasing scrutiny year over year.

**Abu Dhabi Agriculture and Food Safety Authority (ADAFSA)** sets the Emirate of Abu Dhabi's food safety framework, also HACCP-aligned in its expectations.

None of the UAE municipalities require ISO 22000 as a licensing condition. All of them require HACCP-compliant operations.

If municipal licensing is the only driver, HACCP-compliant operations with municipality-approved staff training (e.g. Sharjah Municipality GHP training) is the minimum. If your operation is a single restaurant, this is probably the right level.

## What brand standards require

International hotel brand standards (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, IHG, Accor) typically require ISO 22000 — not just HACCP — for branded properties. The brand standard documentation lists ISO 22000 as the food safety management system requirement.

This is where most UAE hospitality groups find themselves needing ISO 22000. The municipality licensing baseline is HACCP. The brand standard requires ISO 22000. Operators implement ISO 22000, which satisfies both.

## What buyer expectations require

For B2B food operators — central kitchens supplying multiple brands, catering operators bidding for government contracts, food manufacturers selling into supermarkets — buyer audits and tender pre-qualification increasingly specify ISO 22000.

For UAE government tenders involving food (school catering, hospital food, military catering), ISO 22000 is moving from preferred to required in the higher-value contracts.

## The integration most UAE operators end up wanting

For UAE hotels, central kitchens, restaurant groups, and food manufacturers, the engagement that most often makes sense in 2026:

**Integrated FSMS + QMS + EMS** (ISO 22000 + ISO 9001 + ISO 14001):

- ISO 22000 covers food safety with HACCP integrated
- ISO 9001 covers the broader operational quality requirements brand standards expect
- ISO 14001 covers the environmental impact (water, waste, energy) that brand standards and tender bids increasingly score on

One manual covers all three. One internal audit cycle covers all three. One certification body audit covers all three. The integrated engagement is typically 14–16 weeks at 60–70% of the cost of running the three standards separately.

For UAE operators that already hold HACCP-compliant operations under municipality oversight, the documentation lift to ISO 22000 is moderate — the HACCP study is already done, the PRPs are in place, the operational discipline is established. What ISO 22000 adds is the management system documentation, the internal audit cycle, and the certification body audit.

## The Sharjah Municipality GHP question

A specific UAE question we often get: does Sharjah Municipality–approved GHP training satisfy ISO 22000's training requirements?

Yes — with documentation. ISO 22000 requires that food handlers are trained in the food safety competencies relevant to their role. Sharjah Municipality–approved GHP training covers the baseline food handler competencies and is acceptable evidence for ISO 22000 internal and external audits, provided the training records, certificates, and competency assessments are documented and retained.

For managers and supervisors, additional ISO 22000-specific training (food safety team leadership, HACCP study, management system requirements) is typically required on top.

## The five-line decision guide

1. **Single restaurant, municipality licensing only**: HACCP-compliant operations + municipality-approved staff training. ISO 22000 is optional.
2. **Multi-outlet restaurant group, expansion plans**: ISO 22000 worth implementing. Brand-standard alignment, scalable across new outlets, defensible during buyer audits.
3. **Branded hotel (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, IHG, Accor, etc.)**: ISO 22000 required by brand standard. Implement integrated FSMS + QMS where the brand expects ISO 9001 too.
4. **Central kitchen serving multiple brands**: ISO 22000 + ISO 9001 integrated. The central kitchen is the master site; downstream outlets ride the FSMS as annexes.
5. **Food manufacturer selling into UAE retail or export**: ISO 22000 minimum. Higher-tier retail (Spinneys, Carrefour, Lulu) and export markets often require FSSC 22000 (ISO 22000 plus specific PRP requirements) — ask before you commit.
