The second-most-common question on a first call, after cost, is timeline. The answer most UAE operators want is shorter than the answer that survives Stage 2 audit. This is the realistic version.

## TL;DR

For a single-site UAE operation certifying a single standard in 2026:

| Standard | Typical timeline | Constraint |
|---|---|---|
| **ISO 9001** (QMS) | 8–12 weeks | Documentation depth + internal audit cadence |
| **ISO 14001** (EMS) | 8–12 weeks | Aspect-impact register + legal compliance evaluation |
| **ISO 45001** (OHSMS) | 10–14 weeks | Risk assessment depth across hazard categories |
| **ISO 22000** (FSMS) | 8–12 weeks | HACCP study + PRP verification cycle |
| **ISO 27001** (ISMS) | 10–14 weeks | Risk treatment implementation, not documentation |
| **ISO 13485** (Medical QMS) | 12–16 weeks | Design controls + post-market surveillance |
| **ISO 17025** (Lab) | 16–24 weeks | Method validation + uncertainty estimation |
| **ISO 22301** (BCMS) | 10–14 weeks | Tabletop + functional exercise testing |
| **ISO 50001** (EnMS) | 12–16 weeks | Energy baseline + EnPI data collection |

For integrated multi-standard engagements (e.g. ISO 9001 + 14001 + 45001), add 4–6 weeks to the longest constituent timeline. Most integrated systems land at 14–16 weeks.

## The five phases — and how long each takes

Every engagement runs the same five phases. The depth and document count vary by standard. The structural timeline does not.

### Phase 1 — Gap analysis (weeks 1–2)

A clause-by-clause on-site review of the operation against the standard. Two on-site days plus two days of report writing. Output: a written gap-analysis report with findings logged by ownership and target close-out, plus a fixed-fee proposal for the next phase.

**Where this slips**: it does not. Phase 1 is the most predictable part of the engagement.

### Phase 2 — Documentation (weeks 2–6)

The manual, procedures, and records get written, reviewed and version-controlled. The pacing constraints in this phase are:

- **Existing process documentation maturity**. An operation with strong informal procedures rebuilds them as ISO documentation in 3–4 weeks. A greenfield operation takes 5–6.
- **Bilingual translation**. EN+AR documentation adds 1 week to the documentation phase versus single-language.
- **Sector complexity**. Healthcare (ISO 13485, ISO 15189) and laboratory (ISO 17025) documentation is longer and more specific than hospitality or professional services.

**Where this slips**: client review cycles. Documents drafted by the consultancy need review by the client's operational owners. If the client review takes 2 weeks per document round, the documentation phase runs 8+ weeks. We hold to 3-day client review cycles in our default engagement plan; longer cycles are quoted as variations.

### Phase 3 — Implementation (weeks 4–10)

The manual moves off the page and onto the floor. Awareness training for staff, internal-auditor training for nominated staff, and the start of the working evidence trail (monitoring records, training records, supplier qualification records, change records) that the certification body will audit against.

Phase 3 overlaps phase 2 — implementation begins as documentation is finalised, not after. This is what compresses the overall timeline.

**Where this slips**:

- **Staff awareness uptake**. A workforce with low ISO familiarity needs more awareness training rounds. Construction, manufacturing, and hospitality back-of-house typically need 2–3 awareness cohorts; office-based operations 1 cohort.
- **Evidence trail depth**. ISO 27001 risk treatments and ISO 50001 EnPI data collection are the most common phase-3 elongation factors — both require weeks of accumulated evidence, not just documented procedures.

### Phase 4 — Internal audit (weeks 8–11)

A full internal audit cycle against the standard, with findings logged, corrective actions tracked to closure, and a documented management review at the end.

The internal audit takes 2–4 days for a single-site operation. Corrective actions take 2–3 weeks to close. The management review takes a half-day meeting and a week to minute.

**Where this slips**: corrective action closure. A non-conformity raised in the internal audit needs to be closed before Stage 2. Closure requires the corrective action to be implemented and the implementation to be evidenced for at least 2 weeks. NCs raised late in phase 4 push Stage 2 into a later slot.

### Phase 5 — Certification body audit (weeks 12–14+)

Stage 1: the certification body's auditor reviews the documentation and confirms the management system is ready for the on-site audit. Typically 1–2 days, often a hybrid of remote and on-site review.

Stage 2: the certification body's auditor spends 2–5 days on site (depending on operation size), auditing the management system against the standard. Output: a findings report with non-conformities (if any) and a recommendation on certification.

Non-conformity closure: any major NC blocks certification until closed (typically 30–90 days). Minor NCs are usually closed in parallel with certificate issuance.

**Where this slips**: certification body audit calendar. The IAF-accredited certifiers operating in the UAE — TÜV SÜD, TÜV Rheinland, Bureau Veritas, SGS, DNV, LRQA, Intertek — operate booking calendars 6–12 weeks ahead. Booking the Stage 1 + Stage 2 slots at the start of the engagement (week 1 or 2) is what prevents a 3–4 week delay between phase 4 closure and Stage 2 audit.

## What moves the timeline more than consultancy capacity

The consultancy's capacity is not the binding constraint on most UAE engagements. These four are, in roughly descending order of impact:

### 1. Certification body audit calendar

Book the Stage 1 and Stage 2 slots at kick-off, not at the end of phase 4. This single decision saves 3–4 weeks on most engagements.

### 2. Client review cycles

Documents drafted by the consultancy need client review. A 3-day review cycle keeps the engagement on its 8–12 week pace. A 2-week review cycle pushes the engagement to 14–18 weeks. We hold to 3-day reviews in the engagement plan; variations are quoted.

### 3. Evidence trail accumulation

Some standards require time to accumulate audit evidence. ISO 27001 risk treatments need to be implemented and operating for at least 4 weeks before Stage 2 will pass them. ISO 50001 EnPIs need at least 4–6 weeks of metered data. These are not consultancy-capacity bottlenecks; they are standard-required time minimums.

### 4. Site count

Multi-site operators with a defensible master-and-annex documentation pattern (central master site + smaller annexes per outlet) stay close to single-site timelines. Operators that want every site documented separately add 2–4 weeks per additional site.

## The bilingual question

For UAE engagements with Arabic-speaking operations on the shop floor, kitchen, or warehouse, bilingual EN+AR documentation is the default. The translation work adds approximately 1 week to phase 2.

The translation is not a sequential add-on. We translate in parallel with the English drafting once a document is approved in English. Bilingual documentation does not run engagements past the 8–12 week typical window for most standards.

## Integrated multi-standard engagements

For ISO 9001 + 14001 + 45001 (the integrated QHSE system), or ISO 22000 + ISO 9001 (the FSMS + QMS for hospitality), the integrated engagement typically runs 14–16 weeks — 4–6 weeks longer than the longest constituent standard.

The phase structure stays the same. The documentation phase is longer because more manual and procedure documents are produced. The internal audit phase is longer because the audit cycle covers multiple standards. The certification body audit phase stays the same — one Stage 1 and one Stage 2 cover all integrated standards in one audit.

The integrated timeline is significantly shorter than running the standards sequentially. ISO 9001 + 14001 + 45001 sequentially would take roughly 28–36 weeks across three engagements. Integrated, it lands at 14–16 weeks.

## The two non-negotiable time minimums

Two timeline elements cannot be compressed:

**Operating evidence**. Some ISO requirements require the management system to be operating before it can be audited. Internal audits, management reviews, monitoring records, and corrective action closure all need at least 4 weeks of operating evidence. A management system documented in week 6 cannot pass Stage 2 in week 7. This is why phase 3 (implementation) overlaps phase 2 (documentation) by 4–6 weeks.

**Certification body audit calendar**. The certifier's calendar is set independently of the consultancy. Booking 6–12 weeks ahead is normal. Booking 2–4 weeks ahead is unusual and may not be possible during peak audit seasons (Q1 and Q4 are typically busiest for UAE certifiers).

## How to plan the timeline backwards

If the certification date is fixed (a tender deadline, a buyer audit, a brand standard rollout), plan backwards:

- **Stage 2 audit week**: the target
- **Internal audit complete**: 2–3 weeks before Stage 2
- **Documentation complete**: 4–6 weeks before Stage 2
- **Gap analysis complete**: 8–12 weeks before Stage 2 (single standard), 14–16 weeks (integrated)
- **Engagement kick-off**: 1–2 weeks before gap analysis (proposal acceptance + scope confirmation)

For a Stage 2 audit in week 12, engagement kick-off needs to happen by week 0. For a Stage 2 audit in week 16 (integrated engagement), kick-off needs to happen by week 0–2 at the latest.

If the tender deadline gives less runway than this, the timeline cannot be compressed by paying more — the time minimums are real. The right answer in that case is to scope what is achievable in the available window and have an honest conversation with the tender authority about adjusting the requirement or the deadline.
