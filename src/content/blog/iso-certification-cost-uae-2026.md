The question we get asked most often — usually within the first ten minutes of a first call — is what ISO certification actually costs in the UAE. The honest answer is: it depends on the standard, the sector, the number of sites, and whether you are integrating multiple standards. But the ranges are knowable, and the choices that move the number are knowable too.

This is a 2026 breakdown of what UAE organisations can expect to pay for ISO certification, based on engagements Al Batra has scoped and priced through the first half of the year.

## TL;DR

For a single-site UAE operator certifying a single standard in 2026:

- **Gap analysis (discovery-only)**: AED 5,000–18,000 fixed fee
- **Full implementation**: AED 45,000–120,000 fixed fee
- **Integrated multi-standard (2–3 standards together)**: AED 80,000–210,000 fixed fee
- **Certification body audit fee (paid separately)**: AED 12,000–35,000 depending on operation size, standard, and certifier

These are consultancy fees. They do not include the certification body's audit fee, which you pay separately to an independent accredited certifier.

## Two fees, two parties

ISO certification involves two different organisations and two different fees. They are commonly conflated and frequently mispriced.

**The consultancy** (in this case, Al Batra) implements the management system. We deliver gap analysis, documentation, implementation support, internal audit and certification body audit support. Our fee is a fixed price.

**The certification body** is an independent, IAF-accredited organisation that audits the management system and issues the certificate if it conforms. The certification body's fee is set by the certifier, paid directly by you, and is typically billed in two parts: Stage 1 audit and Stage 2 audit.

A consultancy that issues certificates is operating with a conflict of interest. By accreditation rules, the consultancy and the certifier must be independent. Al Batra is the consultancy; we work alongside any of the major IAF-accredited certifiers operating in the UAE — TÜV SÜD, TÜV Rheinland, Bureau Veritas, SGS, DNV, LRQA, Intertek — but we do not issue certificates.

## Gap analysis: AED 5,000–18,000

A gap analysis is a clause-by-clause on-site audit of your operation against the target ISO standard, delivered as a written report with an indicative implementation plan and a fixed-fee proposal for the next phase.

For a single-site operation, a gap analysis typically takes two on-site days plus two days of report writing. The fee range reflects scope: a single small operation, single standard, lands at the lower end. A multi-site or sector-complex operation (e.g. a hospital, a manufacturing plant with multiple production lines, a multi-emirate hotel group) lands higher.

You commit to nothing further after the gap analysis. The report and the proposal are yours.

## Full implementation: AED 45,000–120,000 single standard

A full ISO implementation engagement is the five-phase model that ends with you holding an issued certificate. It covers gap analysis, documentation, on-site implementation support, internal audit, and certification body audit support (Stage 1 + Stage 2 + non-conformity closure).

Where you land in the range depends on:

- **Site count**: single-site operations sit at the lower end; multi-site operations at the higher end
- **Sector complexity**: hospitality and professional services typically sit lower than manufacturing or healthcare
- **Documentation language**: bilingual EN+AR documentation adds approximately 15–20% to a single-language engagement
- **Existing maturity**: operations with strong existing process documentation move faster than greenfield ISMS or FSMS implementations
- **Standard**: ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 typically sit at the lower end; ISO 13485, ISO 17025, ISO 27001 (with deep risk treatment) sit higher

For 2026 engagements, the median Al Batra fee for a single-site, single-standard implementation is AED 65,000–85,000. The first surveillance audit at 12 months post-certification is included in the fixed fee by default.

## Integrated multi-standard: AED 80,000–210,000

An integrated management system covers two or more standards under one manual, one internal audit cycle, one management review, and one certification body audit.

The most common UAE integrations:

- **QMS + EMS + OHSMS** (ISO 9001 + 14001 + 45001): the QHSE integrated system, particularly common in manufacturing, construction, hospitality and facilities operations
- **FSMS + QMS** (ISO 22000 + ISO 9001): for hotels, central kitchens, and food manufacturers
- **ISMS + PIMS** (ISO 27001 + ISO 27701): for FinTechs and professional services handling regulated personal data
- **EnMS + EMS** (ISO 50001 + ISO 14001): for energy-intensive operators and utilities

Integrated engagements typically cost 60–70% of the sum of the three standalone fees. So a QHSE integration that would be roughly AED 200,000 across three standalone engagements lands at AED 130,000–160,000 integrated.

The savings come from shared documentation (one manual, one set of records), shared audit cycles (one internal audit covers all three standards), and shared certification body audit (one Stage 1 and one Stage 2 across all three).

## Certification body audit fees

The certifier's audit fee is paid directly to the certification body, on top of the consultancy fee. Typical 2026 ranges for UAE operations:

- **Small operation (under 50 staff, single site)**: AED 12,000–18,000 for Stage 1 + Stage 2 combined
- **Mid-size operation (50–250 staff, 1–3 sites)**: AED 18,000–28,000
- **Large operation (250+ staff, multi-site)**: AED 28,000–50,000+

Multi-standard integrated audits typically carry a 20–40% uplift over single-standard fees (not a per-standard multiplier, because the auditor is on-site once for all standards).

Surveillance audits (annual, after certification) are typically 40–60% of the Stage 2 audit fee.

## What's charged separately

The Al Batra fixed fee covers the engagement as scoped. Charged separately:

- **Additional on-site days** beyond the default weekly cadence (typically AED 3,500–5,000 per day, used for multi-site rollouts or accelerated timelines)
- **Translation services** beyond what the standard requires (e.g. supplementary marketing-facing translations of the manual)
- **Bespoke training** beyond the two cohorts (awareness + internal auditor) included by default
- **Post-certification support** beyond the first surveillance — usually scoped as an annual surveillance support package (AED 12,000–25,000)
- **Tender pre-qualification packs** for specific UAE/GCC tender submissions, where the buyer expects bespoke evidence presentation

## What moves the price the most

Three choices move the consultancy fee more than any others.

**Integration scope.** Combining two or three standards in one engagement saves 30–40% versus running them sequentially. If you need ISO 9001 and ISO 45001, do them together.

**Site count.** A multi-site operator with a defensible master-and-annex documentation pattern (central master site, smaller annexes per outlet) keeps the fee close to single-site pricing. A multi-site operator that wants every site documented separately adds 25–40% per additional site.

**Existing maturity.** Operations with strong existing process documentation — even informal — move faster than greenfield. If you have working procedures, even unformatted, share them at gap analysis. The fee comes down.

## What we will not do

Two pricing practices are common in the UAE market that we do not follow:

**Open-ended retainers.** A monthly retainer with vague deliverables. Always replace this with a fixed-fee, scoped engagement.

**Per-document pricing.** A fee per procedure or record. Encourages over-documentation and removes the consultancy's incentive to be lean.

If you have been quoted on either of these models, ask for a fixed-fee proposal with a documented scope. Compare apples to apples.
