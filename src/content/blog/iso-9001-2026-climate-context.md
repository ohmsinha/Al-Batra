  <h4>The short answer</h4>
  <p>The 2026 amendments to ISO 9001 tighten three things — climate-impact context, supplier evidence, and risk-treatment audit trails. Existing certificate holders have a transition window agreed by their certification body. Most well-run UAE operations will need a single afternoon of documentation work; the rest will need to revisit risk registers.</p>
</div>

If your organisation holds an ISO 9001:2015 certificate today, the 2026 amendments arrive as a transition rather than a recertification. The three substantive shifts each pull on a different part of the manual.

## 1. Climate context is now explicit, not implied

Clause 4.1 — Understanding the organisation and its context — has long required you to identify external issues that affect the QMS. The 2026 amendment names climate change as a default candidate. UAE operators with significant cooling load, water dependency, or coastal logistics exposure will need to document how they evaluated climate as an external issue, even if the conclusion is "not material at this time."

> Naming climate explicitly is not a green requirement — it is an audit clarity requirement. The amendment closes a gap auditors have already been raising informally for two years.

## 2. Supplier evidence — from "controlled" to "demonstrably controlled"

Clause 8.4 has been re-worded. Where the 2015 standard asked you to ensure externally provided processes, products and services do not adversely affect the organisation's ability to consistently deliver, the 2026 wording adds that the controls must be evidence-based and traceable on demand.

Practically, this means three things for UAE operators:

- **One.** Vendor questionnaires alone are no longer sufficient — auditors will ask for the underlying records.
- **Two.** Long-tail suppliers — one-off, low-spend — need a documented justification for the lighter-touch control.
- **Three.** Bilingual contracts, common in the UAE, need a single source of truth for the controlling language.

## 3. Risk treatment now needs an audit trail, not a register

The 2015 standard let you describe risks and treatments at a relatively summary level. The 2026 amendment expects an audit trail — when a risk was identified, who decided the treatment, what evidence the decision was based on, and when it was last reviewed.

For most UAE certificate holders, this is the longest of the three pieces of work. It is not a matter of re-writing the risk register — it is a matter of versioning it, attaching minutes and approvals to each entry, and reviewing it at a defensible cadence.

## What to do this quarter

If you hold a current ISO 9001:2015 certificate, three actions cover the 2026 transition:

1. Add climate to the clause 4.1 context analysis and minute the discussion.
2. Audit your top ten suppliers for evidence — not for questionnaires — and document the rationale for any lighter-touch control on the remainder.
3. Version-control the risk register and start dating entries.

The 2026 amendment is not a re-certification. Treat it as one quarter of documentation work, audited at the next surveillance visit.
