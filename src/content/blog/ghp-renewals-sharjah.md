GHP renewals in Sharjah follow a published inspection rhythm — but the documentation expected at the gate has tightened steadily. Kitchen managers preparing for a renewal inspection should expect three things to be looked at in detail: food handler certificates, the daily monitoring record cycle, and pest control records.

## Food handler certificates

Every food handler in the kitchen must hold a valid certificate issued by an approved provider. The certificate is checked against the kitchen's roster — temporary staff and short-notice replacements are common gaps in audit.

## Daily monitoring records

Receiving temperatures, cooking temperatures, hot-holding, cold-holding, cooling cycles and cleaning sign-offs are routinely reviewed for the four weeks preceding the inspection. Missed days are the single most common finding.

## Pest control records

Service reports from a licensed pest control contractor are expected to cover the full year, with trend analysis where there are recurring sightings. Reactive-only contracts are increasingly raising findings.

## Three things to do this week

1. Reconcile the food handler register against current payroll. Any handler without a certificate, or with one expiring within thirty days, needs scheduling.
2. Spot-check the last twenty days of monitoring records. Fill in any defensible gaps — and minute the rest as findings to close before inspection.
3. Pull the last twelve months of pest control reports together and check for an annual trend summary.
