## Where we work

- International hotel groups operating in Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Sharjah
- Central kitchens supplying multi-site restaurant brands
- Catering operators servicing offshore platforms, schools and event venues
- Independent restaurant groups expanding from one outlet to a chain

## The certificates that typically apply

- **ISO 22000** — the international food safety management standard, often combined with HACCP study documentation and the prerequisite programmes the local municipality expects.
- **ISO 9001** — required by international hotel brand standards and an increasing number of UAE government tenders.
- **ISO 14001** — for hotels managing significant water, waste and energy footprints, and for kitchens working towards sustainability claims.
- **ISO 45001** — covering kitchen safety, slips and falls, and evacuation planning across complex operating areas.

## How we engage

Hospitality engagements are usually integrated — a single management system covering food safety, quality, environment and safety, with one manual, one set of internal audits and one management review. We deliver weekly on-site, with the lead consultant present for kitchen walk-throughs, supplier audits and management briefings.

## Specifics worth flagging

- **GHP training** — Al Batra is approved by Sharjah Municipality for GHP training, which is commonly delivered as part of an ISO 22000 engagement.
- **Bilingual documentation** — kitchen-floor procedures and food handler instructions are issued in English and Arabic by default.
- **Multi-site rollout** — central kitchen and outlet operations are usually documented separately and audited as a single scope, to keep certification fees proportionate.
